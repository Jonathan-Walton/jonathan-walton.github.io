Chapter 6 of The Great Gatsby tells a great deal about the ACTUAL story of Jay Gatsby’s life. In reality, Jay Gatsby was born James Gatz. He left home at 16 and worked as a fisherman after quitting University. He then meets Dan Cody, his would-be mentor, by rescuing him from his yacht out at sea. Cody greatly influences his life, and when he dies, Gatsby is in line to accept his inheritance, but loses it to Dan Cody’s wife. Everything Gatsby knows, his empire, is all built on the knowledge he learned from Dan Cody.
