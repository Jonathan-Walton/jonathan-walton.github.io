Chapter 2 of The Great Gatsby illustrates the darker side of the ‘Roaring 20’s’. This chapter covers Tom’s affair with Myrtle Wilson. Nick is taken to the City of Ashes where he is introduced to different illegal activities such as drug use, alcoholic beverages, and homosexuality. This chapter is relevant because it reveals the more ominous side of the "perfect" lives of the people of West & East Egg. For example, this chapter also covers Tom’s affair with Myrtle Wilson, showing the more manipulative side of Tom.

Conflicts

Daisy's Love Triangle

Daisy continues her affair with Gatsby, and the relationship climaxes when Gatsby, Daisy, Tom, Jordan and Nick have lunch at the Buchanan mansion. Gatsby and Daisy's affair is revealed by Tom, and they all go out to a hotel for drinks. An argument ensues between Tom Daisy and Gatsby where Daisy is forced to choose between the two while Tom reveals Gatsby's past. Daisy admits she never loved Tom, and is torn between choosing the two. Ensuing the situation afterwards, Myrtle is stuck and killed by Gatsby's car and the blame is placed on him, but he reveals that Daisy was driving, but will continue to take the blame.

Themes

Love is blind, but it doesn't always go both ways.

Gatsby further proves his unwavering loyalty to Daisy by waiting outside till 4am for her. When Nick finds him hiding in the Buchanan's bushes, he says he is waiting in case Tom hurts Daisy or until Daisy signals for him. Although Nick overhears Daisy and Tom reconciling, he hides this fact from Gatsby for he is far too infatuated with the idea of her.
